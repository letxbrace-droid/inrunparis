# Non-Digital Ad Format Guidelines

Evaluation criteria, specs, and best practices for advertising formats beyond digital platforms. Use alongside `ad-platforms.md` (which covers digital) for multi-channel campaign reviews.

---

## Radio & Audio Advertising

### Formats

| Format | Duration | Context |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Radio spot (standard) | 15s, 30s, 60s | AM/FM broadcast, between programming |
| Podcast ad — host-read | 30-90s | Mid-roll or pre-roll, read by host in their style |
| Podcast ad — produced | 15-60s | Pre-produced spot inserted dynamically |
| Streaming audio (Spotify, etc.) | 15-30s | Between songs, unskippable on free tier |
| Jingle / sonic branding | 3-10s | Brand identifier, often end of spot |
| On-hold messaging | 30-60s | Phone hold systems |

### Radio Spot Structure

Standard structure for an effective radio ad:

```
[HOOK — 3-5s]     Attention-grabbing opening. Sound effect, bold statement, question.
[BODY — 15-40s]   Value proposition, proof points, emotional connection.
                   For 30s spots: one message only. For 60s: story arc.
[CTA — 5-10s]     What to do: call number, visit URL, use promo code.
[TAG — 2-3s]      Brand name, jingle, legal disclaimer if required.
```

### Audio Evaluation Dimensions

The 10-dimension framework adjusts for audio:

| Standard Dimension | Audio Equivalent | Notes |
|-------------------|-----------------|-------|
| Attention / Hook | **Opening Impact** | Does the first 3 seconds demand attention? Sound effect, voice tone change, bold claim? |
| Brand Presence | **Brand Audio Identity** | Jingle, sonic logo, brand name mentioned (within 10s and at close). Distinct voice talent. |
| Message Clarity | **Single-Listen Clarity** | Can the listener understand and remember the message after one listen? Radio doesn't allow re-reading. |
| Platform Compliance | **Format Compliance** | Correct duration, appropriate for daypart/programming context, legal disclaimers included if required. |
| Visual Quality | **Production Quality** | Voice talent quality, recording/mixing quality, sound design, music bed appropriateness. No audio artifacts or clipping. |
| CTA Effectiveness | **Audio CTA** | Is the CTA memorable? Phone numbers should be repeated. URLs should be simple. Promo codes should be phonetically clear. |
| Emotional Resonance | **Emotional Tone** | Voice performance, music selection, storytelling. Comedy, urgency, warmth, or authority — does the tone match the brand? |
| Accessibility | **Auditory Clarity** | Clear enunciation, appropriate pace (~150 words/minute for comprehension), no overlapping voice/music, volume consistency. |
| Cultural Fit | **Linguistic Fit** | Accent, dialect, slang appropriate for the market. Humor lands culturally. Music genre fits the audience. |
| Creative System | **Variation Strategy** | Multiple versions testing different hooks/voices/music? Daypart-specific versions? Seasonal rotations? |

### Audio-Specific Anti-Patterns

1. **DJ Voice Syndrome** — Announcer voice that sounds like every other radio ad. Distinctive voice talent matters.
2. **URL Overload** — Giving a complex URL verbally ("visit w-w-w dot brand dash name dot com slash promo slash spring-2026"). Use vanity URLs or promo codes instead.
3. **Sound Wallpaper** — Music bed so prominent it drowns the message. Voice should always be 6-12dB above music.
4. **Speed Reading** — Cramming too much copy into the duration. At 150 wpm: 15s = ~38 words, 30s = ~75 words, 60s = ~150 words. Exceeding this sacrifices clarity.
5. **No Sonic Branding** — Ad ends with no distinctive audio signature. Jingles increase brand recall by 2-3x vs. spoken brand name alone.
6. **Disclaimer Dump** — Legal disclaimers read at triple speed at the end, creating a negative last impression.

### Word Count Guide

| Duration | Max Words | Comfortable Words | Notes |
|----------|----------|------------------|-------|
| 15s | 40 | 30-35 | One statement + brand + CTA |
| 30s | 80 | 65-75 | One message with supporting detail |
| 60s | 160 | 130-150 | Story arc possible, two proof points max |
| 90s (podcast) | 240 | 200-220 | Conversational, host-read style |

## Out-of-Home (OOH) Advertising

### Formats

| Format | Typical Size | Viewing Distance | Dwell Time |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|------------|
| Billboard (standard) | 14' × 48' (US) / 12m × 3m (metric) | 200-500 feet | 3-5 seconds |
| Billboard (digital/DOOH) | Varies | 200-500 feet | 6-8 seconds (rotating) |
| Junior poster | 6' × 12' | 50-200 feet | 5-10 seconds |
| Bus shelter | ~4' × 6' | 10-30 feet | 15-60 seconds (waiting) |
| Transit (bus/metro wrap) | Full vehicle | 10-100 feet | 3-30 seconds |
| Airport/mall | Varies | 10-50 feet | 5-60 seconds |
| Street furniture / kiosk | ~2' × 4' | 5-15 feet | 10-30 seconds |
| Wallscape / building wrap | Building-scale | 100-1000 feet | 5-15 seconds |

### The 3-Second Rule

Billboards must communicate in 3 seconds or less. This is the fundamental constraint:
- **Maximum 7 words** for the headline (some practitioners say 5)
- **One image, one message, one brand**
- No body copy. No paragraphs. No lists.
- Text must be readable at 200+ feet (minimum 1 foot of letter height per 30 feet of viewing distance)

### OOH Evaluation Dimensions

| Standard Dimension | OOH Equivalent | Notes |
|-------------------|---------------|-------|
| Attention / Hook | **Visual Impact** | Bold, simple, high contrast. Does it pop against the urban environment? Tested at thumbnail size. |
| Brand Presence | **Brand Visibility** | Logo prominent but not dominant (~10-15% of layout). Brand colors used as primary palette. |
| Message Clarity | **Instant Read** | 7 words max. Message understood without stopping. No ambiguity. |
| Platform Compliance | **Format Compliance** | Correct dimensions, bleed margins, viewing distance readability, weather/lighting durability. |
| Visual Quality | **Production Quality** | High-resolution (300 DPI at print size), CMYK color, weather-resistant materials considered. |
| CTA Effectiveness | **Action Simplicity** | Limited to: URL (short), phone number, QR code, or "at [store name]". Most OOH has no direct CTA — brand recall IS the action. |
| Emotional Resonance | **Environmental Impact** | Does it create a moment? Clever use of location, lighting, 3D elements, or context. |
| Cultural Fit | **Local Relevance** | Location-specific messaging performs 15-20% better. Language, imagery, and references match the local context. |

### OOH-Specific Anti-Patterns

1. **The Novel** — Too much text on a billboard. If it takes more than 3 seconds to read, it fails.
2. **Tiny Logo Syndrome** — Logo so small it's invisible from viewing distance. Must be recognizable at 200+ feet.
3. **Low Contrast** — Light text on medium background, or colors that wash out in sunlight. OOH needs the highest contrast of any medium.
4. **Digital Slideshow** — DOOH creative that's just a PowerPoint slideshow. Each frame needs to work independently in 6-8 seconds.
5. **Illegible Font** — Script, thin, or decorative fonts that become unreadable at distance. Use bold sans-serif for OOH.
6. **Cluttered Composition** — Multiple products, multiple messages, multiple CTAs. Billboard is a single-idea medium.
7. **QR Code Only** — QR code on a highway billboard. Drivers can't scan at 60mph. QR works on bus shelters and transit, not moving-traffic billboards.

### OOH Typography Minimum Sizes

| Viewing Distance | Minimum Letter Height |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| 100 feet (30m) | 3.3 inches (8.5cm) |
| 200 feet (60m) | 6.7 inches (17cm) |
| 300 feet (90m) | 10 inches (25cm) |
| 500 feet (150m) | 16.7 inches (42cm) |

**Rule of thumb:** 1 inch of letter height per 30 feet of viewing distance.

## Print Advertising

### Formats

| Format | Common Sizes | Context |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Magazine full page | 8.5" × 11" (US) / A4 (metric) | Full-bleed, high production value |
| Magazine spread | 17" × 11" (US) | Two facing pages, maximum impact |
| Newspaper full page | ~11.5" × 21" (broadsheet) | Lower production quality, newsprint |
| Newspaper half/quarter | Various | Cost-effective, frequency play |
| Flyer / leaflet | A5, A4, letter, tri-fold | Handed out, posted, mailed |
| Direct mail | Various (postcard to A4) | Personalized, targeted, envelope or naked |
| Brochure | Tri-fold, bi-fold, booklet | Detailed product/service information |
| Point-of-sale (POS) | Varies (counter cards, shelf talkers) | In-store, at moment of purchase decision |

### Print Evaluation Dimensions

| Standard Dimension | Print Equivalent | Notes |
|-------------------|-----------------|-------|
| Attention / Hook | **Page-Turn Impact** | Does this stop the reader when flipping through? Bold imagery, provocative headline, visual surprise. |
| Brand Presence | **Brand System** | Logo, colors, typography consistent with brand identity. Brand recognizable even at a glance. |
| Message Clarity | **Copy Hierarchy** | Headline → subheadline → body → CTA flow clear. Z-pattern or F-pattern reading honored. |
| Platform Compliance | **Production Specs** | Correct bleed (typically 3mm/0.125"), trim marks, CMYK color mode, resolution (300 DPI), proper file format. |
| Visual Quality | **Production Value** | Photography quality, retouching, typography craftsmanship, paper stock choice (if known). |
| CTA Effectiveness | **Response Mechanism** | URL, QR code, phone number, coupon code, store locator. Print CTAs must be simple and permanent (no expiring links). |
| Emotional Resonance | **Storytelling** | Print allows longer engagement than digital — is the opportunity used? Sequential narrative, intriguing imagery, emotional copy. |

### Print-Specific Anti-Patterns

1. **Digital-to-Print Crop** — Taking a digital ad and cropping it for print without redesigning. Different medium, different reading behavior.
2. **RGB Colors** — Designing in RGB instead of CMYK. Colors shift dramatically (especially blues and greens) when printed.
3. **No Bleed** — White borders from missing bleed area. Looks amateur.
4. **Web Typography** — Using screen-optimized fonts (system sans-serif) instead of print-optimized typefaces. Print allows and rewards finer typography.
5. **Body Copy Desert** — No body copy at all in a medium where people are willing to read. Print is the one medium where longer copy can work.
6. **Coupon Clutter** — Direct mail with 10 competing offers. One primary offer, one CTA.

## Television Advertising

### Formats

| Format | Duration | Context |
|--------|----------|---------|
| TV commercial (standard) | 15s, 30s, 60s | Linear TV and CTV/OTT |
| Long-form | 90s, 2min, 5min | Special placements, Super Bowl, online extensions |
| Infomercial | 30min, 60min | Direct response, usually late-night/cable |
| Bumper / ident | 5-10s | Channel sponsorship, "brought to you by" |
| Connected TV (CTV) | 15-30s | Streaming platforms, often non-skippable |

### TV Commercial Structure

Classic structures for different durations:

**15-second spot:**
```
[HOOK — 2-3s]    Single strong visual or statement
[MESSAGE — 7-8s]  One benefit, one proof point
[CTA+TAG — 4-5s]  Brand logo, tagline, CTA
```

**30-second spot:**
```
[HOOK — 3-5s]     Problem setup or attention-grabbing opening
[BUILD — 10-12s]  Story development, emotional connection, product demo
[RESOLVE — 8-10s] Solution/payoff, brand reveal if not yet shown
[CTA+TAG — 5-7s]  Call to action, brand logo, tagline, sonic logo
```

**60-second spot:**
```
[HOOK — 5-8s]     World-building, character introduction, or bold opening
[DEVELOP — 20-25s] Story arc, tension, emotional journey
[TURN — 10-15s]   Reveal, resolution, product connection
[CTA+TAG — 10-12s] Brand moment, call to action, tagline, sonic logo
```

### TV Evaluation Dimensions

| Standard Dimension | TV Equivalent | Notes |
|-------------------|--------------|-------|
| Attention / Hook | **Opening Seconds** | Does it survive the remote control? With DVR and second-screen behavior, the first 3-5 seconds must hook. |
| Brand Presence | **Brand Integration** | Brand introduced within first 5 seconds AND reinforced at close. Both visual (logo) and audio (name/jingle). |
| Message Clarity | **Story-Message Fit** | Does the story serve the brand message, or does the story overshadow it? Can the viewer recall what was being sold? |
| Platform Compliance | **Format Structure** | Correct duration to the frame. Standard TV has strict timing (29.97s for "30-second" spot). Broadcast standards compliance. |
| Visual Quality | **Production Values** | Cinematography, lighting, color grading, VFX quality, casting, wardrobe, set design. TV has the highest production bar. |
| CTA Effectiveness | **End Frame** | Clear brand moment, memorable sign-off, call to action if direct response. Super/end card legible and on-screen long enough. |
| Emotional Resonance | **Storytelling Arc** | TV is the storytelling medium. Does it create tension and resolution? Does the viewer feel something? |

### TV-Specific Anti-Patterns

1. **The Logo Sandwich** — Opens with logo, closes with logo, no story in between. Viewers tune out pure branding.
2. **Beautiful Irrelevance** — Stunning cinematography that has nothing to do with the product. Wins awards, fails recall.
3. **Punchline Brand Reveal** — Brand only appears in the last 2 seconds. 60% of viewers have already looked away. Research shows early branding outperforms late branding.
4. **Fast-Talking Legal** — Speed-reading disclaimers that make the brand feel untrustworthy. If legal disclosures need 8 seconds of speed-reading, rethink the claim.
5. **Digital Repurposing** — Running a vertical social ad as a TV spot with pillarboxing (black bars on sides). Different medium, different production.
6. **No Sonic Identity** — TV is the one medium where audio is (usually) on. No jingle, no sonic logo, no distinctive music = missed branding opportunity.

## Experiential & Ambient Advertising

### Formats

| Format | Context |
|--------|---------|
| Event booth / activation | Trade shows, festivals, brand pop-ups |
| Pop-up store / experience | Temporary branded retail space |
| Guerrilla / ambient | Unconventional placement — street art, projections, branded utility |
| Sampling | Product trial distribution |
| Branded content / sponsorship | Content that is the ad (events, shows, series) |
| In-store / point-of-sale | Shelf displays, end caps, digital screens at checkout |

### Experiential Evaluation Principles

Experiential advertising is harder to review from creative assets alone (photos, decks, video of the activation). Focus on:

1. **Is there a shareable moment?** The best experiential ads create moments people photograph/record and share. Was the experience designed for earned media?
2. **Brand integration vs. brand interruption** — Does the brand enhance the experience, or is the experience a vehicle for brand messaging?
3. **Conversion path** — Does the experience lead somewhere? (QR code, signup, download, purchase, social follow)
4. **Scalability** — Is this a one-time event or can the concept scale across markets/events?
5. **Memorability** — Will participants remember the brand 30 days later?

## Format-Specific Weight Adjustments

When evaluating non-digital formats, adjust the 10-dimension weights:

| Dimension | Digital (default) | Radio/Audio | OOH/Billboard | Print | TV |
|-----------|------------------|-------------|---------------|-------|----|
| Attention/Hook | 15% | 15% | 20% | 12% | 15% |
| Brand Presence | 12% | 15% | 15% | 12% | 15% |
| Message Clarity | 15% | 18% | 20% | 15% | 12% |
| Format Compliance | 10% | 10% | 8% | 12% | 12% |
| Production Quality | 10% | 15% | 8% | 12% | 18% |
| CTA Effectiveness | 12% | 10% | 3% | 10% | 8% |
| Emotional Resonance | 8% | 10% | 5% | 10% | 15% |
| Accessibility | 6% | 5% | 8% | 5% | 3% |
| Cultural Fit | 7% | 7% | 8% | 7% | 7% |
| Creative System | 5% | 5% | 5% | 5% | 5% |

Key weight shifts:
- **Radio:** Production quality and message clarity go up (single-listen comprehension is everything)
- **OOH:** Attention and message clarity dominate (3-second viewing window), CTA nearly irrelevant
- **Print:** Format compliance matters more (bleed, CMYK, resolution), emotional resonance gets room (longer dwell time)
- **TV:** Production quality and emotional resonance peak (it's the storytelling medium with the highest production bar)
