# Ad Creative Review — Methodology Reference

## Foundational Research

Creative quality is the single largest driver of advertising effectiveness:
- **47% of sales lift** comes from creative quality (Nielsen)
- **56% of ROI** from digital advertising is attributable to creative (Google/Meta studies)
- Emotionally-led campaigns outperform rational ones **2x on profit metrics** (IPA databank, 1,400+ case studies)
- The first **1-3 seconds** determine whether the rest of the ad is seen (Meta, TikTok)
- Ads communicating **one clear idea** consistently outperform multi-message creatives

## Industry Frameworks Referenced

### Meta ABCD Framework (Video/Display)
- **A**ttention — Hook in first 1-3 seconds with bold visuals, surprise, or direct address
- **B**randing — Brand identifiable within 3 seconds, even on mute
- **C**onnection — Emotional resonance through storytelling, humor, or human faces
- **D**irection — Clear CTA with urgency

### Kantar Creative Effectiveness (Link AI)
Scores on five dimensions against a normative database of 200,000+ tested ads:
- **Enjoyment** — Is it pleasant/entertaining to watch?
- **Relevance** — Does it feel relevant to the viewer's life?
- **Brand Cue** — Is there a moment/element that makes the brand memorable? (Strongest predictor of long-term growth)
- **Persuasion** — Does it make the viewer more likely to choose the brand?
- **Distinctiveness** — Does it stand out from other ads?

### System1 Star Rating
Rates ads 1-5 stars based on:
- **Emotional response intensity** (happiness, surprise, etc.)
- **Brand fluency** (can the viewer correctly identify the brand?)
- Key finding: 5-star "famous" ads are **10x more efficient** at driving market share than 1-star ads.

### Nielsen Creative Evaluation
Five dimensions: Attention, Brand connection, Message communication, Emotional engagement, CTA effectiveness.

### IPA Effectiveness Ladder
Ranks creative strategies by business impact:
1. Awareness (lowest impact per £ spent)
2. Differentiation
3. Response / lead generation
4. Behavior change
5. Fame — ads that become cultural talking points (highest impact per £ spent)

## 10-Dimension Scoring Framework

Each dimension is scored 1-5. Weighted total produces an overall creative score out of 100.

### Dimension 1: Attention / Hook (Weight: 15%)

Does the creative earn attention in the first 1-3 seconds?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Immediate pattern interrupt. Impossible to scroll past. Bold visual, provocative statement, or unexpected motion. |
| 4 | Strong opening that creates curiosity or emotional pull. Most viewers would pause. |
| 3 | Decent first impression but not distinctive. Competes adequately in feed. |
| 2 | Weak opening. Logo animation, slow fade-in, or generic imagery. Easy to scroll past. |
| 1 | No hook strategy. Blank/loading first frame, or content that looks like every other ad. |

**Benchmarks:** Thumb-stop rate (3s video views ÷ impressions): >40% excellent, 30-40% good, 20-30% average, <20% poor.

**Hook types to check for:**
- Question hook ("Tired of...?")
- Bold claim hook ("This changed everything")
- Problem agitation hook (show the pain point)
- Social proof hook ("500K users already...")
- Pattern interrupt hook (unexpected visual/sound)
- UGC/testimonial hook (real person speaking to camera)

### Dimension 2: Brand Presence (Weight: 12%)

Is the brand identifiable quickly and consistently?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Brand unmistakable within 2 seconds. Distinctive assets (color, logo, character, visual style) used consistently. Brand is part of the value, not just a stamp. |
| 4 | Brand visible early and integrated naturally. Logo and colors present without dominating. |
| 3 | Brand present but not prominent. Logo visible but could be missed. Colors used inconsistently. |
| 2 | Brand appears late or is barely visible. Could be mistaken for a different brand. |
| 1 | No brand presence, or brand only appears as a tiny logo in the corner. Unbranded experience. |

**Key signals:** Logo size (5-10% of frame is optimal), position (consistent across set), timing in video (must appear within 3 seconds), distinctive brand assets used.

### Dimension 3: Message Clarity (Weight: 15%)

Is the value proposition instantly understandable?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | One clear, compelling message that anyone can understand in 3 seconds. Benefit-focused, not feature-focused. |
| 4 | Clear primary message with supporting detail. Viewer knows what the product does and why they should care. |
| 3 | Message is present but requires effort to parse. Too much text, competing messages, or unclear benefit. |
| 2 | Confused messaging — multiple ideas competing, or message buried in visual noise. |
| 1 | No discernible message. Viewer cannot tell what is being advertised or why they should care. |

**Common failure modes:** Feature listing instead of benefit communication, multiple messages competing, jargon/technical language for general audiences, message only in audio with no text backup.

### Dimension 4: Platform Compliance (Weight: 10%)

Does the creative meet platform specs and best practices?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Perfect platform-native execution. Correct aspect ratio, safe zones respected, text within limits, format matches placement. Feels like it was built for this platform. |
| 4 | Compliant with minor issues. Correct specs but doesn't fully leverage platform conventions. |
| 3 | Mostly compliant but with notable issues — text near safe zone edges, slightly wrong aspect ratio, borderline text overlay. |
| 2 | Clear spec violations — wrong aspect ratio for placement, text in UI overlay zones, oversized file. |
| 1 | Completely wrong format. Landscape in vertical placement, illegible text, broken layout. |

**Pass/fail gates:** Wrong aspect ratio for stated placement is an automatic fail. Safe zone violations for Stories/Reels/TikTok are automatic fails. These should be flagged as CRITICAL regardless of other scores.

### Dimension 5: Visual Quality (Weight: 10%)

Is the creative professionally executed?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Excellent production. Strong composition, intentional lighting, cohesive color palette, professional typography. Authentic imagery that builds trust. |
| 4 | Good production quality. Clean design, readable typography, quality photography/video. Minor imperfections. |
| 3 | Adequate quality. Functional but not impressive. Some stock imagery, basic design, acceptable typography. |
| 2 | Noticeable quality issues — low resolution, poor composition, amateurish design, obvious stock photos, visible AI artifacts. |
| 1 | Unprofessional. Pixelated images, broken layouts, clashing design elements, clearly AI-generated faces. Damages brand credibility. |

**AI-generated content detection signals:** Unnatural hand/finger rendering, inconsistent shadows, garbled text in images, overly smooth skin, background degradation at edges, asymmetric accessories, impossible architectural details.

### Dimension 6: CTA Effectiveness (Weight: 12%)

Does the creative drive action?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Prominent, clear, action-oriented CTA that matches the campaign objective and funnel stage. Creates urgency or desire. Platform CTA matches creative CTA. |
| 4 | Clear CTA that communicates the next step. Visible and appropriately placed. |
| 3 | CTA present but not prominent. Generic ("Learn More" when "Shop Now" is appropriate), small, or poorly positioned. |
| 2 | CTA barely visible, mismatched with objective, or competes with other elements. |
| 1 | No CTA. The viewer has no idea what action to take. |

**Funnel-appropriate CTAs:**
- Awareness: "Learn More", "Watch Now", "Discover"
- Consideration: "See How", "Compare", "Get the Guide"
- Conversion: "Shop Now", "Buy Today", "Get Started", "Download"
- Retargeting: "Come Back", "Complete Your Order", urgency-based

### Dimension 7: Emotional Resonance (Weight: 8%)

Does the creative connect emotionally?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Strong emotional impact — viewer feels something (joy, surprise, recognition, aspiration, relief). Uses human faces, storytelling, or relatable scenarios effectively. |
| 4 | Emotional connection present. Relatable content or appealing imagery that creates positive association. |
| 3 | Neutral — informational but not emotionally engaging. Functional but forgettable. |
| 2 | Emotionally flat or slightly negative. Cold, corporate, or disconnected from the audience's reality. |
| 1 | Emotionally off-putting. Inauthentic, tone-deaf, or actively unpleasant. |

**Key principle:** Human faces with eye contact increase engagement. Real people outperform models/stock. Emotion > information for long-term brand building.

### Dimension 8: Accessibility & Readability (Weight: 6%)

Can everyone consume this creative?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Captions on all video, high-contrast text, readable at mobile sizes (40px+ at 1080w), clear visual hierarchy, inclusive representation. |
| 4 | Most accessibility basics covered. Readable text, good contrast, captions present. |
| 3 | Partially accessible. Some text too small, contrast issues in places, no captions on video. |
| 2 | Significant readability issues. Small text, low contrast, no captions, cluttered layout. |
| 1 | Illegible on mobile. Critical information is unreadable, no accommodation for sound-off viewing. |

**85% rule:** 85% of social feed video is watched on mute (Meta data). Any video ad without captions or text overlays is failing 85% of its audience.

### Dimension 9: Cultural Fit & Localization (Weight: 7%)

Is the creative appropriate and effective for its target market?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Transcreated — adapted for cultural context, not just translated. Uses locally relevant imagery, humor, references. Feels made for this market. |
| 4 | Well-localized. Proper translation, culturally appropriate imagery, correct currency/formatting. |
| 3 | Adequate localization. Translation is correct but literal. Imagery is generic but inoffensive. |
| 2 | Poor localization. Mixed languages, awkward translation, culturally mismatched imagery. |
| 1 | Offensive or completely inappropriate for the market. Wrong language, cultural missteps, untranslated elements. |

**Cultural check signals:**
- Mixed languages within one creative (e.g., English CTA on a Hindi ad)
- Text overflow from translation length differences (German/French ~20-30% longer than English)
- Color meanings across cultures (red = luck in China vs. danger in West)
- Hand gestures, physical representation, food/drink sensitivity
- Right-to-left layout needs for Arabic/Hebrew/Urdu markets

### Dimension 10: Creative System & Testing (Weight: 5%)

Does the creative set work as a testing/optimization system?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Structured testing framework visible — clear hook/body/CTA variations, concept diversity, format variety per placement, fatigue management plan. |
| 4 | Some structured variation. Multiple concepts tested, format-appropriate assets, some variation in hooks/CTAs. |
| 3 | Limited variation. A few different concepts but no systematic testing. Same CTA across all creatives. |
| 2 | Near-duplicate creatives. Same layout with minor photo swaps. No meaningful testing surface. |
| 1 | Single creative or identical batch. No testing strategy. One concept, one format, one angle. |

**Creative velocity benchmark:** Top-performing advertisers produce 5-10 new variations per week per campaign (Meta/TikTok case studies).

## Overall Score Calculation

```
Overall = (Attention × 0.15) + (Brand × 0.12) + (Message × 0.15) + (Platform × 0.10)
        + (Visual × 0.10) + (CTA × 0.12) + (Emotion × 0.08) + (Accessibility × 0.06)
        + (Cultural × 0.07) + (System × 0.05)

Scale: Score out of 5 → multiply by 20 for 0-100 score

Rating:
  90-100: Exceptional — world-class creative
  75-89:  Strong — competitive, minor improvements
  60-74:  Average — functional but significant opportunity
  45-59:  Below Average — multiple issues suppressing performance
  30-44:  Weak — fundamental problems, creative overhaul needed
  <30:    Critical — actively damaging brand/wasting spend
```

**Weight adjustments by campaign type:**
- **Awareness campaigns:** Increase Emotion to 12%, decrease CTA to 8%
- **Direct response/conversion:** Increase CTA to 15%, increase Message to 18%, decrease Emotion to 5%
- **Multi-market campaigns:** Increase Cultural to 12%, decrease System to 3%
- **Brand campaigns:** Increase Brand to 15%, increase Emotion to 12%, decrease Platform to 7%

**Weight adjustments by medium** (see `references/ad-formats.md` for full table):
- **Radio/Audio:** Visual Quality becomes Production Quality (voice talent, mixing, sound design). Single-listen clarity is critical.
- **OOH/Billboard:** Attention (20%) and Message Clarity (20%) dominate. CTA drops to 3%. Three-second viewing window.
- **Print:** Format Compliance matters more (bleed, CMYK, resolution). Longer dwell time allows emotional resonance.
- **Television:** Production Quality (18%) and Emotional Resonance (15%) peak. TV is the storytelling medium with the highest production bar.

## Anti-Pattern Catalog

### Strategic Anti-Patterns

**1. Funnel Blindness**
Running the same creative to all audiences regardless of funnel stage. Cold audiences need education and hook; warm audiences need urgency and offer.
- **Detection:** All creatives have the same CTA and messaging angle
- **Impact:** Wastes spend on mismatched creative-audience combinations
- **Fix:** Create distinct creative sets per funnel stage with appropriate messaging

**2. One-Concept Syndrome**
Entire creative library is variations on a single concept. No meaningful test of different angles, hooks, or value propositions.
- **Detection:** All creatives have the same layout/structure with minor photo/text swaps
- **Impact:** No learning about what resonates. Creative fatigue hits the entire set at once.
- **Fix:** Test 3-5 genuinely different concepts before producing variations of a winner

**3. Brand-First, Audience-Second**
Leading with brand messaging ("We are proud to announce...") instead of audience-relevant hooks ("Tired of X?").
- **Detection:** Opening frames focus on logo, brand name, or corporate messaging
- **Impact:** Fails the 1-3 second attention test. Brand message never reaches the viewer.
- **Fix:** Lead with the audience's problem or desire. Brand reveals after the hook lands.

**4. Creative-Landing Page Disconnect**
Ad promises one thing, landing page delivers another.
- **Detection:** Compare ad messaging/imagery with stated destination
- **Impact:** Kills post-click conversion even when ad performs well
- **Fix:** Ensure visual and message continuity from ad to destination

### Execution Anti-Patterns

**5. Text Wall**
Too much text on the creative. Trying to fit a paragraph into a 1080×1080 frame.
- **Detection:** Text covers >30% of creative area, or >3 text elements compete
- **Impact:** Nothing is read because nothing stands out. Mobile users scroll past.
- **Fix:** One headline, one supporting line maximum. Let the visual do the work.

**6. Invisible CTA**
CTA that doesn't contrast with the surrounding design, or is too small, or is positioned outside the visual flow.
- **Detection:** CTA blends into background, is <10% of creative area, or is in a corner
- **Impact:** Viewer sees the ad but doesn't know what to do next
- **Fix:** CTA should be the highest-contrast element. Prominent, action-oriented, appropriately sized.

**7. Silent Treatment**
Video ad where important information is conveyed through audio only, with no captions or text overlays.
- **Detection:** Video ad with speaking but no captions/subtitles/text
- **Impact:** 85% of feed video viewers watch on mute — they get nothing
- **Fix:** Add captions/text overlays that communicate the message visually

**8. Stock Photo Graveyard**
Generic stock photography that could belong to any brand in any category.
- **Detection:** "Business people shaking hands," "person on laptop," "diverse meeting room"
- **Impact:** Zero distinctiveness. Viewer can't remember which brand this was for.
- **Fix:** Use authentic brand-specific imagery. Real customers, real product, real situations.

**9. AI Uncanny Valley**
AI-generated people, products, or scenes that look synthetic.
- **Detection:** Unnatural hands/fingers, inconsistent shadows, garbled text in images, overly smooth skin
- **Impact:** Erodes trust, especially with authenticity-sensitive audiences
- **Fix:** Use AI for ideation/mockups. Use real imagery for production creatives.

### Localization Anti-Patterns

**10. Translation Not Transcreation**
Direct word-for-word translation that loses cultural context, humor, or emotional resonance.
- **Detection:** Copy feels stilted, uses formal structures where colloquial would be more effective, humor/wordplay doesn't land
- **Impact:** Ad feels foreign to the audience. Reduced emotional connection.
- **Fix:** Hire native-market copywriters who adapt intent, not just words.

**11. Franken-Language**
Mixed languages within one creative — e.g., local-language headline with English CTA, or UI screenshots in a different language than the ad.
- **Detection:** Two or more languages visible in the same creative without clear intent
- **Impact:** Signals low effort. Breaks immersion. Confuses the viewer.
- **Fix:** Every text element in the target language. All UI screenshots localized.

**12. Template Rigidity**
Forcing a global template that doesn't work for a specific market's text length, reading direction, or visual norms.
- **Detection:** Text overflow/truncation, cramped layouts, design that worked in English but breaks in German/Arabic
- **Impact:** Poor readability, unprofessional appearance
- **Fix:** Design flexible templates that accommodate ±30% text length variation. Create RTL variants for Arabic/Hebrew/Urdu markets.

## Creative Testing Framework Assessment

When evaluating whether a brand's creative set reflects a structured testing approach, check for:

### Hook/Body/CTA Variation Matrix

A well-structured test separates variables:
- **Hook test:** 3-5 different hooks with same body and CTA
- **Body test:** Same hook, 3 different value propositions/proof points, same CTA
- **CTA test:** Same hook and body, 3 different calls-to-action

This enables **combinatorial testing**: 5 hooks × 3 bodies × 3 CTAs = 45 meaningful permutations.

**Signs of structured testing:**
- Creatives that share elements but vary one dimension
- Clear naming conventions (e.g., "Hook_Problem_CTA_ShopNow")
- Multiple concepts genuinely different from each other
- Format-appropriate assets (9:16 for Stories, 1:1 for Feed, etc.)

**Signs of unstructured creative:**
- Random naming ("Group 14.png", "final_v3_revised.jpg")
- All creatives look nearly identical with minor photo swaps
- Same concept recycled across months with no evolution
- No format adaptation (same creative cropped for different placements)

### Creative Fatigue Assessment

- **Frequency threshold:** Performance degrades after frequency 3-4 (cold audiences), 6-8 (retargeting)
- **Leading fatigue indicators:** CTR declining week-over-week (>20% over 2 weeks), CPM rising while CTR falls, negative feedback increasing
- **Refresh cadence:** High-spend campaigns need fresh creative every 2-4 weeks. Low-spend can sustain 4-8 weeks.

## Report Writing Guidelines

### Severity Ratings

- **CRITICAL** — Actively wasting spend or damaging brand. Fix immediately. (Examples: wrong aspect ratio, no CTA, offensive cultural content, AI-generated faces for trust-dependent product)
- **HIGH** — Significantly suppressing performance. Fix in next creative round. (Examples: weak hook, message overload, poor localization, brand inconsistency)
- **MEDIUM** — Clear improvement opportunity. Plan for future rounds. (Examples: generic stock photos, suboptimal text sizing, missing captions on video)
- **LOW** — Polish item. Competitive advantage if fixed. (Examples: minor brand color drift, CTA copy optimization, slightly cluttered layout)

### Cross-Cutting Findings Format

Each cross-cutting finding should include:
1. **Title** — Clear, specific naming of the issue
2. **Severity** — CRITICAL / HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW
3. **Affected creatives** — Which specific ads exhibit this issue (with thumbnails)
4. **What's happening** — Observable evidence
5. **Why it matters** — Impact on the primary KPI, with research/data support
6. **What to do** — Specific, actionable recommendation with feasibility context
7. **Priority** — ICE score (Impact × Confidence × Ease) for implementation ordering
